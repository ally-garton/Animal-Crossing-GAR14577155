History of the Series

Animal Crossing is a community simulation video game series developed and published by Nintendo. The premise of the game is based on a human player living in a village inhabited by anthropomorphic animals, and carrying out various activities including fishing, bug catching, and fossil hunting. The series has been noted for its open-ended gameplay and use of the Nintendo consoles' internal clock and calendar to simulate real passage of time.

The original Animal Crossing was released in Japan in 2001, with the most recent releases being spin-offs released in 2015. A mobile app spin-off has recently been developed, due for release on the 22nd of November 2017.

  • Animal Crossing, Nintendo GameCube, September 2004

  • Animal Crossing: Wild World, Nintendo DS, March 2006

  • Animal Crossing: City Folk, Nintendo Wii, December 2008

  • Animal Crossing: New Leaf, Nintendo 3DS, June 2013

  • Animal Crossing: Happy Home Designer, Nintendo 3DS, October 2015

  • Animal Crossing: Amiibo Festival, Nintendo WiiU, November 2015
